The Process

From zero
to selling.

01
Reach Out to Upline
Contact your upline to get connected with the team and join the JG Financial Discord — the hub for community, training, and support. They'll walk you through the process, answer questions, and make sure you start in the right place.
Step Zero
Join the Discord →
02
Pre-Licensing
Complete your Life pre-licensing course online via Xcel Solutions. Use code 2020 at checkout.
$30 with code 2020
xcelsolutions.com →
03
State Exam & Fingerprints
Schedule your proctored state exam and complete your fingerprint requirements. Bring your Xcel certification and two forms of ID. 150 questions, 70% to pass. Some states require fingerprints before testing — check the State Guide for your state's specifics.
$40–$100 per attempt
State guide →
04
License Application
Once you pass your state exam, apply for your resident license through your state's Department of Insurance — every state has its own portal, links are in the State Guide. NIPR is used later for non-resident licenses. Approval takes 5–10 business days, then you'll receive your NPN.
State fee + $5.60
State guide →
05
Carrier Contracting + E&O
With your NPN confirmed, apply for carrier appointments through SuranceBay (SureLC). E&O Insurance is obtained inside SureLC during contracting — required before most carriers appoint you. Heartland carriers contracted manually through your upline.
Contracting FREE · E&O $127 + $27/mo
Agent Portal →
06
Start Selling
You're a fully licensed, contracted JG Financial agent. Plug into training, pull leads, run your first appointments, and start protecting families. Your manager will get you up to speed on dialers, scripts, and the JG sales process — everything you need to start producing.

Frequently Asked

Answers to the
questions we get most.

What is JG Financial?
JG Financial is a national life insurance agency. We partner with top-rated carriers and provide our agents with mentorship, training, contracts, and the systems they need to build a real career — whether they're brand new or already in the industry.
Do I need experience to join?
No. We work with both licensed agents and people who've never sold insurance before. If you're not licensed yet, we'll walk you through the entire process step by step.
Do I need a license to apply?
You don't need to be licensed to apply or get approved. Once approved, you'll go through our licensing process: pre-licensing via Xcel ($30 with code 2020), state exam, NIPR application, then carrier contracting.
How long does licensing take?
Most agents are fully licensed and contracted in 2–4 weeks. Pre-licensing is self-paced through Xcel, so motivated agents move faster. NIPR approval typically takes 5–10 business days.
How much does it cost to get started?
Roughly $230–$330 total: $30 pre-licensing, $40–$100 state exam, varies for state license fee, FREE carrier contracting, and $127 down + $27/mo for E&O insurance.
What carriers do you work with?
Four carrier groups: UIG (American Amicable, AHL, F&G, Mutual of Omaha, Transamerica), Supreme (Instabrain, SBLI, Foresters), Ethos (online appointment), and Heartland (Baltimore Life, United Home Life, Polish Falcons — manual contracting).
How do agents get paid?
Agents are paid commission directly by the carriers. Contract levels are competitive and tied to production. No salary, no caps — your income is based on what you sell. Day-one vested residuals on every policy.
Is this remote or in-person?
JG Financial agents work both ways. The agency operates nationally with mentorship and training delivered virtually, and agents run appointments either virtually or in-person depending on their market.
What kind of training and support do you offer?
Direct 1:1 mentorship from a manager, group training, scripts and presentations, a CRM and dialer system, lead sources, and back-office support for everything from contracting to claims.
How do I apply?
Reach out to Julian directly at julianmginsurance@gmail.com. We'll set up a quick conversation, walk you through the process, and answer any questions before you commit.
